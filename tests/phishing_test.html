Millions of Apple Users Urged to Verify Account After Credential Leak

By James Worthington  ·  March 3, 2026  ·  4 min read

A new wave of credential harvesting campaigns is targeting Apple, Google, and Microsoft account holders, according to researchers at cybersecurity firm SentryLabs. The attackers leverage typosquatted domains and urgency-driven language to push victims into re-entering their username and password on convincing lookalike pages.

Security experts warn that the campaigns typically begin with a convincing notification — often an email or browser alert — claiming the user's account has been suspended due to unusual activity. Recipients are instructed to verify their account immediately by clicking a link and re-entering their Apple ID or Google credentials, including their password and sometimes their credit card details for "billing verification."

"These pages are increasingly difficult to distinguish from the real thing," noted SentryLabs analyst Dr. Clara Prentiss. "The URLs are the primary giveaway — look carefully. A domain like apple-security.accountverify.net or accounts.google-confirm.xyz is not affiliated with Apple or Google, even if the page looks identical."

The Federal Trade Commission issued guidance last month urging users to never enter account credentials on a page reached via an unsolicited link. If you receive a message claiming your account is locked or compromised, navigate directly to the official website rather than clicking embedded links.

To protect yourself: enable two-factor authentication on all accounts, use a password manager, and be especially cautious of messages creating a sense of urgency — phrases like "act now or your account will be deleted" or "your subscription will expire within 24 hours" are classic red flags.
